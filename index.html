SafePedestrianGo

Gotta catch ‘em all? With SafePedestrianGo, you won’t get caught in oncoming traffic

What is SafePedestrianGo?

When the first cell phone call was made over 40 years ago, there was no possible way to predict the impact cellular technology would have on the world. With the rise of the smartphone, what began as a simple source of mobile communication soon evolved into a norm of constant interconnectivity. However, the advent of these pocket computers inevitably comes with risks, as the ability to interact with the outside world through a handheld device can be dangerously distracting. The rise of mobile AR only exacerbates the issue, causing people to unwittingly put themselves in unsafe situations in the pursuit of new experiences (or rare Pokémon). SafePedestrianGo allows us to combat this danger – by using computer vision and map data, we can detect if pedestrians are looking at their phones while approaching a road. A notification will then pop up to notify the user so that they look up and are aware of any oncoming traffic.

Why Does it Matter?

Innovations in smartphone technology have indisputably enhanced our lives, but along with these technological advancements comes a responsibility to address the growing problem of “inattentional blindness.” The recent mobile phenomenon Pokémon Go (alongside other apps) has stolen the attention of people across the globe, leading to a spike in pedestrian injuries. We were inspired to develop a solution to an issue that is causing tangible harm to tech enthusiasts. According to a Wall Street Journal article written by columnist Gregory A. Fowler, “emergency room visits involving distracted pedestrians using cellphones were up 124% in 2014 from 2010.” We hope to see this number decline with the introduction of SafePedestrianGo and similar initiatives focused on the safety of those traveling by foot in urban areas.
